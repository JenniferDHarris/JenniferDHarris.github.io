Today in class we reviewed Pastor Josh's sermon on The Upper Room. Pastor Josh focused on John 18:1-27 which recounts Jesus's arrest and questioning by the Jewish authorities.

Jesus was calm despite the circumstances. He demonstrated control and authority, even in the face of opposition. Peter was in a state of fear and panic. He denied knowing Jesus three times, just as Jesus had said he would. Jesus is "I AM"; Peter is "I am not". Yet, despite Peter's shortcomings, he is considered one of Jesus's most prominent and influential disciples. Peter went on to play a significant role in the early church. This teaches us that our life is about dependency, not performance. I think Charles Stanley said it best - "Christ in us assumes the responsibility for the performance of everything He requires of us." Let's not let past failures get in the way of our current relationship with Jesus.

"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come; The old has gone, the new is here!" 2 Corinthians 5:17
